Aircraft Logistics Management is a Django application built for the Air Force Logistics team Tesseract. My team of three built this project from scratch, from the design to the functionality. This aircraft maintenance application required heavy lifting to schedule a fleet of aircrafts, keeping track of when the next tune-up is needed, viewing upcoming maintenance and deployment on the calendar, and managing inventory for parts.

Project Purpose and Goal

This project involved three key phases of development. In the initial phase, my team and I focused on understanding the specific needs of the Air Force organizers. We engaged closely with the organizers to refine the application's design through iterative feedback. During the second phase, we employed Agile methodology to manage the project's lifecycle. By conducting regular sprints and maintaining close communication with the team. In the final phase, we conducted a thorough walk-through of the application with the organizers. Based on their feedback, we implemented a highly requested feature, showcasing our commitment to delivering the best project for the Air Force.

Web Stack and Explanation

Django was the optimal choice for our web application due to its scalability, essential for deployment across numerous Air Force bases. Python played a crucial role in extracting old maintenance logs and integrating them into various database tables. Additionally, adhering to the DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself) principle enabled us to efficiently develop and complete the application within a tight timeframe.

Problems and Thought Process

Like most projects, We ran into a few bumps along the way, one particularly difficult area was the organization of the old maintenance document to be integrated into the database. Requiring an algorithmic solution to parse through unnecessary data points within the document. Lessons Learned

I could spend all day describing the lessons that I learned while working on this project, but the most important ones involved my newfound understanding of the Django framework. As my first large project using Django, I learned a lot of lessons regarding code structure and organization. Understanding the various phases of the development process was particularly beneficial, especially when using the Agile methodology.
