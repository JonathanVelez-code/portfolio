    Tesseract AF Aircraft Maintenance Project

    Tesseract the Air Force Office of Logistics Innovation got connected with the UCF...

    Read More
    Project: Link
    Project Organizer: Kris Lopez - Email or LinkedIn, Georgia Riggs - Email or LinkedIn, Philip Barry - Email.

    Project Overiew

    Tesseract the Air Force Office of Logistics Innovation got connected with the UCF Computer Science Department to develop an Aircraft Maintenance Web Application. The primary objective was to improve the management of Air Force base maintenance logs, addressing inefficiencies inherent in the existing data maintenance process. The web application, meticulously designed for intuitive user experience, offers seamless navigation for selecting specific base locations. Upon selection, users are presented with a dynamic calendar showcasing scheduled aircraft maintenance, tailored to organizers' specifications for each day. The drag-and-drop functionality empowers users to effortlessly modify maintenance dates, enhancing flexibility and responsiveness to operational needs. An additional page features a comprehensive list of aircraft approaching maintenance thresholds, enabling users to proactively schedule maintenance by simply clicking on the desired aircraft and seamlessly integrating it into the calendar. This forward-thinking approach not only optimizes aircraft upkeep but also recognizes the critical importance of timely maintenance for pilot safety, encompassing both important maintenance and routine aircraft maintenance protocols. Furthermore, Tesseract's Aircraft Maintenance Web Application facilitates the seamless importation of Excel documentation, ensuring a smooth transition from conventional record-keeping methods to this state-of-the-art digital solution.

    Challenges Faced

    Leading the resolution of a critical issue related to the creation of the parser. Anticipating potential roadblocks months in advance, I proactively alerted my team to the complexities that could arise from disparate formatting and terminology across various Air Force base locations. In response, I devised an innovative solution rooted in the implementation of two distinct types of Excel documents—a weekly and a monthly maintenance sheet. The parser systematically scours the Excel sheets, extracting essential Aircraft Maintenance information. Recognizing the inherent variability in data completeness, I ensured a seamless process by incorporating a mechanism for manual entry, responding to specific requests from organizers. Once the parser completes its formatting, it not only prevents redundant data entry but also efficiently updates essential information for each aircraft. The successful implementation of this solution garnered positive feedback from organizers who appreciated the pragmatic approach taken to address the unique challenges presented by the diverse dataset. This accomplishment underscores my ability to devise innovative solutions, navigate complex technical landscapes, and deliver impactful results under challenging circumstances.

    Skills Learned

    The position of Lead Frontend Developer was given based on previous experience, I led a dynamic team of frontend and API developers, providing valuable guidance on essential project requirements and creation. In this role, I honed my skills through in-depth research on the complexities of designing a user-friendly website, implementing robust security protocols for the frontend, and adhering to industry standards to ensure the project's scalability for future enhancements. This experience not only showcased my ability to lead and collaborate effectively but also underscored my commitment to staying up-to-date with cutting-edge practices in web development.

    Results and Impact

    Enhanced the operational efficiency of all Air Force bases by implementing a standardized format for Aircraft maintenance information. Successfully addressed critical challenges related to scalability, time management, and user-friendliness. Prior to the project, limitations included a single user editing the Excel sheet at a time, necessitating specialized training for terminology comprehension, and adherence to stringent rules encompassing color coding and formatting. The transformative impact of this initiative not only streamlined processes but also significantly improved accessibility and ease of use. The organizers were pleased with the results and commitment of the team.

    Project Overiew

    The Restaurant Rating Web Application offers users a comprehensive platform for searching and discovering new restaurants. Users can leverage the platform to explore a wide array of dining options based on location, cuisine preferences, and user-generated reviews. The search functionality allows users to filter results according to various criteria such as price range, ratings, and specific amenities. the Review system empowers users to make informed decisions by reading firsthand experiences and ratings from fellow patrons. Additionally, users can access photos, menus, and contact information, providing a holistic view of each restaurant.

    Challenges Faced

    A primary challenge in this project was mastering the MERN stack—an endeavor that marked my initial exposure to Node.js, Express.js, and Jest. Delving into this technology stack was essential for the successful implementation of user security features, a pivotal aspect of the project's requirements.

    Skills Learned

    Mastering the MERN stack during this project has significantly augmented my proficiency in subsequent endeavors, providing a foundation for tackling diverse challenges. This experience has not only expanded my technical skill set but also reinforced the importance of meticulous unit testing. Recognizing the critical role of unit testing in software development, I comprehensively integrated Jest to validate and ensure the reliability of the implemented features, promoting code quality and minimizing potential issues. Furthermore, this project marked a pivotal milestone as one of the initial large-scale collaborative initiatives in which I actively participated. Working within a group setting enhanced my ability to collaborate effectively, fostering teamwork and communication skills essential for successful project execution.

    Results

    The culmination of the project yielded a resounding success as our team successfully executed and delivered all project features. In a culmination presentation before our peers and professor, we showcased the depth and effectiveness of our work. Our comprehensive demonstration thoughtful questions from the audience, demonstrating their engagement and interest in our project. The positive feedback and praise received underscored the project's commitment to delivering a high-quality product.

    Most photographers find it hard to see interesting pictures in places in which they are most familiar. A trip somewhere new seems always exactly what our photography needed, as shooting away from home consistently inspires us to new artistic heights.

    Streets empty that are usually busy are remarkable and can evoke the sense of historical pictures from before the invention of the motorcar. Other things that are different at the moment will be queues to get into stores and the lines marked out on the floor to show how far apart we should be.

    Pretend everything is new and that you haven’t seen it before, and then you will be free to notice the leading lines, the places where one edge meets another in delightful geometric harmony, and how the ordinary things in the kitchen are transformed when the light is on or off.

    The trick here is to look slowly, and then look again. Take the time to look in detail and to look at the same thing from different angles, with different light, long lenses and wide lenses. Then move to the left a bit. You may never feel the need to leave the house again.
